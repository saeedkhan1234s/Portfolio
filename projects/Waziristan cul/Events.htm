Introduction

Waziristan, a region in northwest Pakistan, has a long and unique cultural heritage. The thesis begins by describing the socio-political and economic organisation of the tribes in Waziristan in the mid-nineteenth century. Attention is drawn to the egalitarian ethos and the importance of tarburwaliâ€”rivalry between patrilateral parallel cousins.

Relations between the tribes and the British authorities during this period are explored, focusing on both conflict and cooperation. A major issue was the tension along the south Waziristan border caused by Mahsud raiding.

In conclusion, the thesis analyses British frontier policy and its influences. In particular, ideas about how the tribes were organised and how they could be managed are discussed, along with the techniques used in tribal management.
